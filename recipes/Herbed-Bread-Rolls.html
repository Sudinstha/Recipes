Herbed Bread Rolls

Description

In this recipe, small, bun-sized pieces of herbed and yeasted dough are arranged in a quiche pan or shallow cake pan and baked. They come to the table joined together in a singular circular cluster, and diners can each break off their own roll. Make 18 rolls.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon dried yeast
  • about 1¼ cups warm water
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • a3 cups unbleached plain(white) bread flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 teaspoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh oregano leaves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • ½ yellow asafetida powder
  • poppy seeds, sesame seeds, fine oatmeal, or dried herbs for topping

Recipe Steps

  1. Combine yeast, a few teaspoons of warm water, and the sugar in a small bowl, Set a side for 10 minutes, or until the mixture is frothy.
  2. Whisk together flour and salt.
  3. Add oil, herbs, and asafetida to the frothy yeast mixture.
  4. Make a well in the center of the flour and pour in the yeast mixture. Add three-quarters of the warm water and mix. Add enough of the remaining for 5 minutes or until smooth. water, if required, to make a soft but nonsticky dough. Knead the dough
  5. Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and leave in a warm place for one hour or until doubled in size.
  6. Punch down the dough and divide into 18 even-sized pieces. Shape them by rolling them around under cupped hands on a floured board or marble slab, then arrange them fairly close together in a lightly oiled 25-cm (10-inch) quiche pan or round, shallow baking dish. Keep in mind that the rolls will approximately double in size.
  7. Allow the rolls to rise again for about 30 minutes. Spray with water and sprinkle with poppy seeds, sesame, fine oatmeal, or dried herbs.
  8. Preheat the oven to 220°C / 430°F and bake the rolls for 30–35 minutes or until golden brown and hollow-sounding when tapped on the underside. Serve.
